# Escalation Policy

Use this function to configure automatic escalation policies that ensure tickets receive appropriate attention when SLA targets are at risk or when tickets remain unaddressed.

Escalation policies automatically escalate tickets to higher-level support or management when specific conditions are met, ensuring timely resolution and preventing SLA breaches.

In the main window, click **Admin > Service Desk > Escalation Policy**. The Escalation Policy window displays.

## New Escalation Policy

From the *Select Actions* drop-down list, choose **New Escalation Policy**. The New Escalation Policy window displays.

Enter a *Name* and *Description* for this policy.

Configure the **Policy Settings**:

**Is Active**: Check to enable this policy.

**Applies To**:

- Select ticket types (Incident, Request, Problem, Change)
- Optionally filter by category, priority, or assignment group

### Define Escalation Levels

Click **Add Escalation Level** to create each escalation tier.

For each escalation level, configure:

**Level Order**: Sequence of this escalation (Level 1, Level 2, etc.).

**Trigger Conditions**:

Choose what triggers this escalation level:

- **Time-Based**: Escalate after X hours/days
  - Since ticket creation
  - Since last update
  - Before SLA breach (e.g., when 80% of SLA time has elapsed)
  
- **State-Based**: Escalate when ticket is in specific state for X time
  
- **Priority-Based**: Different timings based on priority
  
- **No Response**: Escalate if no response from assignee

**Escalation Time**: Enter the time threshold.

**Escalation Actions**:

Select actions to perform when escalated:

**Notify**:

- Click **Add** to select users, roles, or groups to notify
- Options: Assignee's manager, VP of IT, Escalation team
- Notification method: Email, SMS, In-app

**Reassign**:

- Auto-reassign to senior technician
- Reassign to escalation group
- Reassign to specific user

**Increase Priority**:

- Automatically increase priority by one level
- Set to specific priority

**Update Fields**:

- Add escalation flag
- Update custom fields
- Add escalation comments

**Create Task**:

- Generate escalation review task
- Assign to management

**Trigger Workflow**:

- Execute specific workflow
- Send to executive review

Click **Save** to add this escalation level.

### Add Multiple Escalation Levels

Continue adding escalation levels to create a multi-tier escalation chain.

Example escalation chain:

**Level 1** (After 2 hours with no response):
- Notify assignee's manager
- Add comment to ticket

**Level 2** (After 4 hours or at 80% of SLA):
- Reassign to senior support team
- Notify service desk manager
- Increase priority

**Level 3** (At 95% of SLA or after 6 hours):
- Notify VP of IT
- Escalate to executive team
- Set priority to Critical

When all escalation levels are configured, click **Save**.

## Common Escalation Scenarios

### SLA-Based Escalation

- **Trigger**: When 75% of SLA time has elapsed
- **Action**: Notify assignment group manager

### Unassigned Ticket Escalation

- **Trigger**: Ticket unassigned for more than 30 minutes
- **Action**: Notify service desk supervisor

### High Priority Escalation

- **Trigger**: P1 ticket open for more than 1 hour
- **Action**: Notify executive team

### Stale Ticket Escalation

- **Trigger**: No updates in 48 hours
- **Action**: Reassign to different technician

## Edit Escalation Policy

Navigate to the window containing the policy to edit.

Click the line containing the policy. The policy details window displays.

Make modifications to:

- Policy settings
- Escalation levels
- Trigger conditions
- Actions

Click **Save**.

## Activate/Deactivate Policy

To activate a policy:

Select the policy.

From the *Select Actions* drop-down list, choose **Activate**.

To deactivate:

Select an active policy.

From the *Select Actions* drop-down list, choose **Deactivate**.

## Clone Escalation Policy

To create a new policy based on an existing one:

Select a policy.

From the *Select Actions* drop-down list, choose **Clone Policy**.

Enter a new name and make modifications as needed.

Click **Add**.

## Delete Escalation Policy

Click the line that contains the policy to delete.

From the *Select Actions* drop-down list, choose **Delete**.

## View Escalated Tickets

To view all currently escalated tickets:

Click **View Escalated Tickets**.

The window displays all tickets that have been escalated, along with:

- Escalation level
- Time escalated
- Reason for escalation
- Actions taken

## Escalation Reports

To view escalation metrics:

Click **Escalation Reports**.

Available reports:

- Escalation frequency by category
- Escalation trends over time
- Average escalation level
- Tickets escalated to management
- Escalation resolution times

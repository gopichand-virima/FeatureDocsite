# Audits

The [Audits]{.Strong} tab in the CMDB allows users to define and manage audit schedules for Configuration Items (CIs). Audits ensure the **integrity and accuracy** of CI data through periodic checks.

![Configuration Management Database 65](../Resources/Images/CMDB - Left-Nav/Configuration Management Database_65.png)

- Click on [New Audit]{.Strong} to add new audit.

- Enter the details, then click [Add]{.Strong} to save the new audit.

- Select one audit and click [Delete]{.Strong} to delete the audit.

[Key functionalities:]{.Strong}

- [Audit name]{.Strong}: Assign a meaningful label to indicate the audit\'s purpose (e.g., \"detail check,\" \"OS validation\").

- [Add description]{.Strong}: Describe the audit\'s scope or objective.

- [Assign auditor]{.Strong}: Designate a user responsible for executing or reviewing the audit. Multiple users can be assigned.

- [Set recurring frequency]{.Strong}: Choose how often the audit should recur (daily, weekly, monthly, etc.).

- [Duration (in days)]{.Strong}: Specify the audit\'s validity duration or completion window.

[Example scenario:]{.Strong}

For an audit named ["detail check"]{.Strong}:

- It will run [daily]{.Strong}.

- It has a [duration of 7 days]{.Strong}.

- The assigned auditor is [Auditor Name]{.Strong}.

- The audit\'s description is [\"check details.\"]{.Strong}

This setup enables timely verification of asset configurations and promotes regular CI hygiene practices.

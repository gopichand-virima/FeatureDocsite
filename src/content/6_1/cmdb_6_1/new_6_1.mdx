# New

The <span class="Strong" style="font-weight: bold;">New</span> action is used to manually create a new CI entry in the CMDB.

1.  Click the <span class="Strong" style="font-weight: bold;">Select Actions</span> dropdown at the top-right of the CMDB list.

2.  Choose <span class="Strong" style="font-weight: bold;">New</span> from the dropdown.

    <span class="image placeholder" original-image-src="../Resources/Images/CMDB%20-%20Left-Nav/Configuration%20Management%20Database_14.png" original-image-title=""></span>

- Users can define the <span class="Strong">CI type</span> (based on a blueprint) and manually fill in its properties.

  <span class="image placeholder" original-image-src="../Resources/Images/CMDB%20-%20Left-Nav/Configuration%20Management%20Database_15.png" original-image-title=""></span>

<!-- -->

- This is helpful when an asset isn't discovered automatically or needs to be added proactively.

- The form will include fields like Asset Name, Host Name, Status, Serial Number, etc., based on the selected blueprint.

- Enter the details, then click <span class="Strong" style="font-weight: bold;">Add</span>.

  <span class="image placeholder" original-image-src="../Resources/Images/CMDB%20-%20Left-Nav/Configuration%20Management%20Database_16.png" original-image-title=""></span>

Great for creating standalone or exception assets not brought in through discovery or integrations.

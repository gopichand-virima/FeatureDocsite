# Risk Assessments

Risk Assessment provides a flexible way to capture information from the Assignee in order to derive the risk of the associated change. Libraries of questions can be used to derive the risk of a change based on criteria contained within the change. For example, a different set of questions could be set for a hardware change versus a software change.

The assessment uses a weighted score approach for each question. The overall score for an assessment is evaluated against thresholds to determine the risk of a change.

In the main window, click **Admin > Change Management > Risk Assessments**. The Risk Assessments window displays.

!

!


### New Risk Assessment

- From the *Select Actions* drop-down list, choose **New Risk Assessment**. The Risk Assessment window displays.

!

- Enter the *Name*, *Introductory Information* and *End Notes*.
- Click **Add**.


### Edit Risk Assessment

Not all functions include an option to edit record line items shown in the main window.


- Navigate to the window containing the record to edit.
- Click the line containing the record. The applicable window or dialog box displays.
- Make the necessary modifications.
- Click **Save** or **Add**, as applicable.
- Complete the other tabs, referring to the sections below.


### Risk Assessment Criteria

Use this function to create risk related questions to assess its criteria.

- In the Details window, click **Risk Assessment Criteria.** A list containing all currently defined questions displays.  
  !  

- Click **New Assessment Question**.

!

- In the *Question* field, enter the risk-related question.
- In the *Weight* field, enter a number for this question.
- Enter a *Value* and assign a *Score*.
- Click the plus + sign to add a new choice.
- Click the minus - sign to remove a choice.
- Click **Add**.


### Risk Assessment Threshold

Use this function to configure a threshold value for the Risk Severity- High, Low, and Medium

- In the Details window, click **Risk Assessment Threshold**. A list of all currently configured thresholds displays.  
  !  

- Click **New Assessment Threshold**.

!

- In the *Risk* field, click the drop-down list and select the applicable risk.
- In the *Score greater than* field, enter the threshold value.
- Click **Add**.

To export the threshold information shown in the window, click **Export**. An Excel spreadsheet is generated and an email is sent when the spreadsheet is available for download.

To delete a threshold, select the line item(s) containing the threshold(s), and click **Delete**.


### Delete Risk Assessment

Deleting is a permanent action and cannot be undone. Deleting may affect other functionality and information in the application such as data in configured reports, fields in windows, selectable options, etc. Therefore, be sure to understand the potential effects before making a deletion.


- Click the line that contains the item to delete.
- From the *Select Actions* drop-down list, choose **Delete**. If a confirmation message is displayed, type **Delete** and click **Delete**to confirm.
